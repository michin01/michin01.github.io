I will be joining the University of Massachusetts Lowell in Fall 2018 as an Assistant Professor! I received my PhD from Washington University in St. Louis and was advised by Dr. Caitlin Kelleher. My work focuses on improving support for novice and end-user programmers programming outside of a classroom context. This work involves the design, development, and testing of tools that leverage available information or resources in order to encourage motivation and learning.

Generally, my research sits between human-computer interaction and learning. My interests include: novice/end-user programming, blocks-based programming environments (like Scratch, App Inventor, Looking Glass, and Alice), example code use, recommending help to programmers, computer science education, and crowdsourcing.

In my free time, I enjoy long distance running, kayaking, playing clarinet, swing dancing, and trying all of the oreo flavors. I have run several half marathons and my first full marathon in March 2018!
